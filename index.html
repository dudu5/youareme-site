I was lost in myself.

Not confused about the world. Lost in myself. Running in circles that felt like thinking. Overwhelmed by the weight of everything I was holding that had no shape.

I couldn't see the pattern because I was inside it.

What changed everything was the work of making it visible.

Not thinking about it harder. Building a map by touching it.

Loading what I was holding — the confusion, the contradictions, the pieces that didn't fit.

Then iterating. Touching it. Adjusting it. Looking at what I just made. Feeling whether it was right. Adjusting again.

Each touch made it more real. Each iteration brought me closer to what was actually true.

I wasn't waiting for insight. I was forging it. Building my map of the territory I was walking, one adjustment at a time.

The process was the clarity.

If this is true for you too, there's a door.
