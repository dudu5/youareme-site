Yet I spent most of my life knowing surprisingly little about what was actually happening inside me.

I knew my opinions. My preferences. My desires. I was my identity.

But the deeper currents — what I actually needed, what drove my reactions, what I was really feeling beneath the performance — that remained largely unexamined.

I never mapped that territory. Not because I didn't care. Because no one showed me how.

And because looking honestly took more courage than staying busy.

What I felt —

— wasn't because I was broken.

It was because I was living with someone I didn't actually know.

Myself.
