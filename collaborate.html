Who I am

I'm just a human like you.

Like you, I'm exploring what it means to be alive.

I spent a decade mapping systems for organizations. I was good at seeing what was broken in others.

But I couldn't see my own.

I was running on autopilot — my family's traumas, my culture's definitions of success. I spent energy on what looked good from outside, not what felt right within. I performed to fit in because I didn't feel safe enough to follow myself.

I worked hard. I achieved. I felt empty.

Around 2023, I hit a wall. I couldn't keep performing.

What I discovered: Understanding alone doesn't reprogram your system. Your body has to experience that change is safe.

I went to coaching school. Meditation school. But what actually changed was this: I turned my observational skills inward. I started mapping myself the way I'd mapped organizations.

My wife and I deepened our practice together through constant honest communication. Through failing, adjusting, trying again.

I'm still in it. Still facing the protective mechanisms my nervous system fires when it senses risk. Still learning to stay present instead of shutting down.

But I'm awake to it now.

And I've learned something essential: When two people who are both awake meet — something real becomes possible that can't happen alone.

You can't think your way out of a wound. But you can be witnessed in it. You can have someone stay present while you learn to choose differently. That changes everything.

This is what I do.

I help you understand your own operating system. Not to transcend it. But to align it. So your automation becomes a partner instead of a prison.

I do this because I believe real change is possible — not because I've transcended it, but because I'm in the middle of it too, awake.

What changes

You increase your agency.

→ From autopilot to choice.
→ From reaction to intention.
→ From performing to being real.
→ From scattered to aligned.

Not because I hand you the answer. But because you finally see what's actually running the show.

I know this because I've lived it. A few years ago, when my deepest wounds triggered, I had no idea what was happening. I'd be stuck for days, sometimes a week. Yesterday, that same wound activated. I didn't see it coming. But I understood what it was about, where it came from, and what the antidote is — because I've met it so many times and stayed with it. That's what got me out of it in two hours.

That's the difference: understanding your pattern changes everything. When you know what triggers you, what happens in you, and how to move through it — you have real choice.

How it works

The first contact

You email me. You tell me where you are and how I can help.

I respond within days, sometimes hours. You get my actual time and presence, not an automated response.

We schedule a call.

The first call

We meet. We explore what it's like to be in interaction with each other. We experience how our energies meet.

This isn't about me giving you a plan. It's about discovering if we want to move forward together. And if we do: what's the next step between us.

What happens next depends on what you need.

But here's what changes: you're no longer working alone.

What a session actually looks like

You log on. We say hello. We check in — how you're actually feeling, where your energy is, what's alive in you right now.

Then you choose how to land.

We can do a very short grounding — just closing our eyes, breathing, feeling your body in your seat. Something to arrive.

Or we can dive straight into what's on your mind.

You tell me where you are. What you're struggling with. What you see ahead. What you want to explore.

I listen. Really listen.

Then I mirror back to you what I hear. You hear yourself through my reflection.

That act — hearing yourself clearly — changes what you can perceive about yourself.

Suddenly the pattern is visible. The choice becomes real.

That's the session.

Ongoing 1:1 work

We work together consistently. You bring what you're noticing about yourself. We reflect on it together.

Between sessions, we use this tool I built. It helps you externalize what you're noticing so when we meet, we're not starting from scratch.

You take small steps, observe what happens, bring it back. Over time, the changes compound. And faster, because you're not hoping you're moving in the right direction — you can see it.

The timeline

Real, meaningful change takes time.

Whether you see shifts in months or years depends entirely on whether you believe in what we're doing — but that doesn't mean you won't notice a difference immediately. If you don't recognize the value, if you don't believe this path is worth walking, then we won't continue. And that's okay.

But if you do believe, if you stay with it, if you keep observing and reflecting — you will change. Not dramatically, but deeply.
